Best Cheap Places to Visit in Europe in December#

Dreaming of a budget-friendly escape from the winter blues? Europe awaits with an array of affordable destinations in December, offering unique experiences and warm hospitality. Here’s a curated guide to some of the best cheap places to visit, ensuring an unforgettable holiday without breaking the bank!

1. Prague, Czech Republic:#

  • Why Prague?: This captivating city boasts stunning architecture, rich history, and a vibrant festive atmosphere during December.

  • Budget-Friendly Finds:

  • Accommodation: Cozy hostels start at a mere $15/night, while budget hotels average around $30-$40/night.

  • Food: Indulge in hearty goulash or traditional Czech dumplings at local restaurants for as low as $10-$15 per meal.

  • Attractions: Explore the stunning Charles Bridge, visit the iconic Prague Castle, and admire the Astronomical Clock, all free of charge.

  • Insider Tip: Don’t miss the Christmas markets, where you can find unique gifts and delectable treats while soaking in the enchanting holiday spirit.

2. Lisbon, Portugal:#

  • Why Lisbon?: Lisbon captivates with its colorful streets, charming trams, and breathtaking views. December offers mild weather, making it ideal for exploration.

  • Budget-Friendly Finds:

  • Accommodation: Hostels offer beds from $15-$20/night, while budget hotels start around $35-$40/night.

  • Food: Savor authentic Portuguese cuisine at local eateries, with meals averaging between $10-$15.

  • Attractions: Ride the iconic Tram 28, visit the majestic São Jorge Castle, and enjoy a stroll through the Alfama district, all without spending a dime.

  • Insider Tip: Take a day trip to nearby Sintra, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its enchanting palaces and gardens.

3. Budapest, Hungary:#

  • Why Budapest?: Budapest is famous for its thermal baths, stunning architecture, and vibrant nightlife. December brings magical Christmas markets and festive events.

  • Budget-Friendly Finds:

  • Accommodation: Hostels offer beds from $10-$15/night, while budget hotels start around $25-$30/night.

  • Food: Treat yourself to traditional Hungarian dishes like goulash or lángos at local restaurants for around $10-$12 per meal.

  • Attractions: Visit the iconic Hungarian Parliament Building, soak in the Széchenyi Thermal Baths, and marvel at the beauty of Buda Castle, all for free.

  • Insider Tip: Take advantage of Budapest’s ruin bars, repurposed from abandoned buildings, offering unique nightlife experiences at budget-friendly prices.

4. Kraków, Poland:#

  • Why Kraków?: Kraków is a charming city steeped in history and culture. December brings enchanting Christmas markets and cozy winter festivals.

  • Budget-Friendly Finds:

  • Accommodation: Hostels offer beds from $12-$15/night, while budget hotels start around $25-$30/night.

  • Food: Enjoy traditional Polish pierogi or hearty bigos at local restaurants for around $7-$10 per meal.

  • Attractions: Explore the beautiful Wawel Royal Castle, venture into the Wieliczka Salt Mine, and wander through the atmospheric Main Market Square, all without spending a penny.

  • Insider Tip: Don’t miss the chance to visit the Auschwitz-Birkenau Memorial and Museum, a somber reminder of history and a testament to the resilience of the human spirit.

5. Athens, Greece:#

  • Why Athens?: Athens is an ancient city that blends historical wonders with modern vibes. December offers mild weather, allowing for comfortable exploration of its iconic landmarks.

  • Budget-Friendly Finds:

  • Accommodation: Hostels offer beds from $10-$15/night, while budget hotels start around $25-$30/night.

  • Food: Delight in authentic Greek dishes like souvlaki or moussaka at local tavernas for around $7-$10 per meal.

  • Attractions: Visit the Acropolis, explore the ancient Agora, and stroll through the picturesque Plaka neighborhood, all without paying an entrance fee.

  • Insider Tip: Take a day trip to nearby Cape Sounion, home to the ruins of the Temple of Poseidon, offering breathtaking views of the Aegean Sea.

Hey there, fellow adventurers! Winter is here, and while some may be hibernating, it’s time for us budget-savvy travelers to embark on a European escapade. December offers a unique charm, with cities adorned in festive decorations, twinkling lights, and a palpable holiday spirit. Plus, with the off-season rates, you can enjoy incredible experiences without burning a hole in your pocket. So, pack your bags, let’s hit the road, and create memories that will warm your heart all winter long.

1. Explore Majestic Museums and Historical Sites:

  • Budapest, Hungary: Marvel at the grandeur of Buda Castle, stroll through the cobbled streets of the Castle District, and immerse yourself in Hungarian history at the Hungarian National Museum.

  • Prague, Czech Republic: Journey through time in Prague’s Old Town Square, visit the iconic Charles Bridge, and unravel the captivating stories of Czech history at the Prague Castle complex.

  • Vienna, Austria: Admire the opulent Schönbrunn Palace, stroll through the enchanting Belvedere Gardens, and immerse yourself in the musical legacy of Vienna at the Haus der Musik.

2. Indulge in Festive Delights:

  • Nuremberg, Germany: Experience the magic of the world-famous Nuremberg Christkindlesmarkt, indulge in gingerbread, mulled wine, and traditional German delicacies.

  • Strasbourg, France: Immerse yourself in the Christkindelsmärik, Strasbourg’s enchanting Christmas market. Savor Alsatian specialties like “bredele” cookies and sip on warm vin chaud.

  • Brussels, Belgium: Discover the Winter Wonders festival, a dazzling spectacle of lights, music, and festive cheer. Indulge in Belgian waffles, hot chocolate, and the irresistible Manneken Pis adorned in a Santa Claus costume.

3. Experience Cultural Treasures:

  • Lisbon, Portugal: Visit the Castelo de São Jorge, wander through the historic Alfama district, and explore the National Tile Museum, showcasing Portugal’s rich ceramic heritage.

  • Copenhagen, Denmark: Immerse yourself in the Design Museum Denmark, marvel at the architectural wonders of Amalienborg Palace, and stroll along the picturesque canals of Nyhavn.

  • Stockholm, Sweden: Explore the Vasa Museum, housing the preserved warship Vasa, wander through the open-air Skansen museum, and soak in the beauty of Stockholm’s archipelago.

Remember, December in Europe is not just about saving money; it’s about embracing the magic of the season, exploring captivating destinations, and creating unforgettable memories. So, pack your sense of adventure, wrap up warm, and let Europe’s winter wonders enchant you.

Local culinary delights and recommendations for affordable dining options#

Europe in December transforms into a winter wonderland. Yes, the weather is chilly, even icy, but look beyond that and you’ll find the cities adorned with dazzling Christmas lights, magical markets, and mulled wine galore.

And yes, Europe can be expensive. But worry not, with a keen eye, you can still travel on a budget and have a blast.

Let’s talk food. Europe is bursting with culinary treasures from pizza in Rome to goulash in Budapest. When you’re on a budget, cheap eats can often be the best food you have, market stalls where locals go, no-frills restaurants serving traditional dishes. Think of a steaming bowl of pho at a Vietnamese cafe in Paris or a pocketful of juicy pork souvlaki in Athens.

Here are a few budget-friendly gems for your next European getaway:

  • Budapest, Hungary: Bundle up and explore the city’s thermal baths and indulge in hearty Hungarian dishes like goulash and lángos (fried flatbread) at Central Market Hall.

  • Prague, Czech Republic: Marvel at the stunning architecture of Old Town Square and warm up with a mug of mulled wine and indulge in traditional Czech dishes like svíčková (beef sirloin in cream sauce) and knedlíky (boiled dumplings) at affordable local pubs.

  • Lisbon, Portugal: Discover Alfama’s charming streets and indulge in local seafood specialities and delectable pastries like pastel de nata (custard tarts) at hole-in-the-wall cafes.

Key Takeaways:

  • Research local markets, street food stalls, and hole-in-the-wall restaurants for budget-friendly dining options.
  • Embrace local cuisine and try traditional dishes at affordable local pubs and cafes.
  • Look for food tours that offer a taste of the local culinary scene at a reasonable price.
  • Take advantage of happy hour specials and early bird menus to save money on dining out.
  • Consider cooking some meals at your accommodation to save even more money.

FAQ#

Q1: What are some of the most affordable destinations in Europe to visit in December?#

A1: Some of the best budget-friendly destinations in Europe for a December getaway include Prague, Czech Republic; Budapest, Hungary; Krakow, Poland; Porto, Portugal; and Sofia, Bulgaria. These cities offer a blend of affordable accommodations, historical attractions, and festive holiday markets.

Q2: Are there any hidden gems in Europe that are particularly charming during December?#

A2: Off-the-beaten-path destinations like Tallinn, Estonia; Sibiu, Romania; and Colmar, France, offer unique experiences and affordable prices. These charming towns boast medieval architecture, festive decorations, and cozy cafes, making them ideal for a magical winter escape.

Q3: What kinds of activities can I enjoy in Europe during December on a budget?#

A3: From ice skating in Vienna’s Rathausplatz to exploring Christmas markets in Strasbourg, Europe offers a range of affordable activities in December. Other budget-friendly options include visiting free museums, attending festive concerts, or simply wandering through the charming streets adorned with holiday lights.

Q4: How can I save money on transportation and accommodation while traveling in Europe in December?#

A4: To save on transportation costs, consider using budget airlines or taking advantage of train passes that offer discounted fares. For accommodation, hostels and budget hotels provide affordable options, while vacation rentals can be a cost-effective choice for groups or families.

Q5: Any tips for finding the best deals on flights and accommodation for a December trip to Europe?#

A5: To secure the best deals, book your flights and accommodation well in advance. Consider flying on weekdays or during off-peak hours to take advantage of lower fares. Additionally, keep an eye out for special promotions and discounts offered by airlines and hotels.
