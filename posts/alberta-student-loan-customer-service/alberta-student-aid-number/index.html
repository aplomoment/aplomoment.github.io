FAQ#

Q1: What is an Alberta Student Aid (ASA) number?#

A1: An Alberta Student Aid (ASA) number is a unique 10-digit identifier assigned to you when you apply for student financial aid through Alberta Student Aid. This number is used to track your application and communicate with you about your financial aid status.

Q2: How do I get an ASA number?#

A2: To get an ASA number, you need to create an account on the Alberta Student Aid website. Once you have created an account, you will be prompted to provide personal information, including your name, address, and Social Insurance Number (SIN). After you have submitted your application, you will be assigned an ASA number.

Q3: Where can I find my ASA number?#

A3: You can find your ASA number on your Alberta Student Aid application confirmation page. You can also find your ASA number on any correspondence you receive from Alberta Student Aid.

Q4: What should I do if I have forgotten my ASA number?#

A4: If you have forgotten your ASA number, you can retrieve it by logging into your Alberta Student Aid account. Once you have logged in, click on the “My Profile” tab and then click on the “My ASA Number” link.

Q5: How can I use my ASA number?#

A5: You can use your ASA number to:

  • Check the status of your student financial aid application
  • View your financial aid award letter
  • Make changes to your application
  • Submit additional documents
  • Contact Alberta Student Aid with questions or concerns
