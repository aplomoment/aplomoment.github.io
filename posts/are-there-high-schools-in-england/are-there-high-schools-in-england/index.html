What is the curriculum in English high schools?#

The curriculum in English high schools is determined by the Department for Education (DfE), which sets out the national curriculum for all state-funded schools in England. It also includes non-academic subjects such as physical education, art, and music.

What are the admissions requirements for English high schools?#

Admissions Requirements for English High Schools: A Closer Look

In England, high schools, also known as secondary schools, typically cater to students aged 11 to 18. While there are no universal admissions criteria, schools can set their own requirements, often influenced by factors like location, academic reputation, and size.

To secure a place in their preferred high school, students typically need to:

  1. Submit a completed application form: This form typically includes basic personal information, academic history, and details of any special needs or circumstances.

  2. Provide academic records: Schools usually request copies of a student’s academic records, including report cards and standardized test scores, to assess their academic performance and potential.

  3. Write a personal statement: Through a personal statement, students have the opportunity to showcase their individuality, aspirations, and suitability for the school. Schools may provide specific prompts or guidelines for this statement.

  4. Obtain letters of recommendation: Letters of recommendation from teachers, counselors, or other professionals who are familiar with the student’s academic achievements and personal qualities can provide valuable insights to the admissions committee.

  5. Take an entrance exam: Some schools may conduct entrance exams to further assess a student’s academic abilities and suitability for their programs. These exams might cover subjects like English, Math, and Science.

The admissions process can be competitive, particularly for highly sought-after schools. Therefore, it’s advisable for students to apply to multiple schools to increase their chances of securing a place.

Remember, the specific admissions requirements for English high schools can vary, so it’s crucial to check the individual school’s website or contact the admissions office for more detailed information.

FAQ#

Q1: What types of high schools are there in England?#

A1: In England, there are several types of high schools, including grammar schools, comprehensive schools, academies, and free schools. Grammar schools are selective schools that admit students based on academic ability, while comprehensive schools are non-selective and open to all students in a particular area. Academies and free schools are independent schools that are publicly funded but operate outside of local authority control.

Q2: What curriculum is taught in English high schools?#

A2: The curriculum in English high schools is determined by the national government and includes core subjects such as English, mathematics, science, and history. Students also have the opportunity to choose from a range of optional subjects, such as foreign languages, arts, and technology.

Q3: What are the admissions requirements for English high schools?#

A3: Admissions requirements for English high schools vary depending on the type of school. Grammar schools typically require students to take an entrance exam, while comprehensive schools do not. Academies and free schools may have their own admissions criteria, such as a lottery system or an interview process.

Q4: What is the history of high schools in England?#

A4: The history of high schools in England can be traced back to the Middle Ages, when grammar schools were established to provide education for boys from wealthy families. In the 19th century, comprehensive schools were introduced to provide education for all students, regardless of their social class. Academies and free schools were introduced in the 21st century as part of a government initiative to improve educational standards.

Q5: How do high schools in England compare to those in other countries?#

A5: English high schools are similar to those in other developed countries in terms of their curriculum and academic standards. However, there are some key differences, such as the emphasis on selective education in England and the greater autonomy of English schools compared to those in some other countries.
