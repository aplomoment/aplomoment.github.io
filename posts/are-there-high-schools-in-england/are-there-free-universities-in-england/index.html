Challenges of Free University Education#

1. It would be expensive.

Free university education would be expensive. The government would have to find a way to pay for it. One way would be to raise taxes. Another way would be to cut funding for other government programs.

2. It could lower the quality of education.

If tuition were free, universities might have to cut costs. This could lead to larger class sizes, fewer faculty members, and less individual attention for students. Education quality might suffer.

If public universities were free, fewer students would attend private colleges and universities. This could hurt these schools financially and lead to them closing their doors.
