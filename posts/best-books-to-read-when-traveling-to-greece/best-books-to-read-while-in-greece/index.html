Best Books to Read While in Greece#

As a seasoned globetrotter with an insatiable literary appetite, I’ve made my way to Greece more times than I can count. Each journey has been an immersion into a captivating whirlpool of history, mythology, and modern-day vibes.

Ancient Greek Masterpieces: A Bridge to the Past#

  1. Homer’s Iliad and Odyssey: Dive into the swirling vortex of epic poetry as you follow the exploits of Achilles and Odysseus. Their journeys are brimming with timeless themes of valor, loyalty, and homecoming.

  2. Sappho’s Poetry: Feel the reverberations of Sappho’s passionate verses, where love and longing dance in every line. Her words paint a vivid portrait of ancient Lesbos.

  3. Aeschylus’ Oresteia Trilogy: Witness the tragic tale of Agamemnon’s family saga, a poignant exploration of justice, vengeance, and the relentless pursuit of redemption.

Literary Gems of Modern Greece#

  1. Nikos Kazantzakis’ Zorba the Greek: Embark on a life-affirming voyage with Zorba, a charismatic Cretan peasant. His zest for life and infectious spirit will leave an indelible mark on your soul.

  2. Stratis Myrivilis’ Life in the Tomb: Immerse yourself in the haunting tale of a group of Greek soldiers trapped in a claustrophobic tomb during World War I. Their struggle for survival and the resilience of the human spirit shine through the darkness.

  3. Yannis Ritsos’ Rembetiko: Dive into the heart of Greece’s vibrant Rembetiko music scene through the eyes of a young man seeking solace and self-discovery.

Contemporary Tales That Capture the Essence of Greece#

  1. Victoria Hislop’s The Island: Unravel the poignant story of a young woman exiled to the remote island of Spinalonga during the early 20th century. Love, loss, and the enduring strength of the human spirit intertwine in this captivating narrative.

  2. Jeffrey Eugenides’ Middlesex: Follow the extraordinary journey of Calliope Stephanides, a Greek-American hermaphrodite, as she navigates identity, love, and the complexities of family.

  3. Amanda Michalopoulou’s I’ll Fly Tomorrow: Feel the pulse of contemporary Athens through the eyes of a struggling young woman, Anna. Her quest for independence and self-discovery reflects the vibrant energy of modern Greece.

As you traverse the best books to read while in Greece, let the stories wash over you like the gentle waves of the Mediterranean Sea. They will transport you to ancient ruins, bustling cities, and the heart of the Greek people.

FAQ#

Q1: What are some iconic classic books set in Greece that capture the country’s historical essence?#

A1: Among the must-read classic books that vividly portray Greece’s rich history and cultural heritage are “The Odyssey” and “The Iliad” by Homer, “The Histories” by Herodotus, and “The Peloponnesian War” by Thucydides. These works provide a glimpse into ancient Greece’s epic tales, legendary battles, and the foundations of Western civilization.

Q2: Can you suggest some contemporary Greek novels that offer a modern perspective on the country’s culture and society?#

A2: For a deeper understanding of contemporary Greece, consider reading novels such as “The Island” by Victoria Hislop, “Zorba the Greek” by Nikos Kazantzakis, and “The Durrells in Corfu” by Gerald Durrell. These books explore themes of identity, family, and the evolving landscape of Greek culture, providing insights into the hearts and minds of modern Greeks.

Q3: Are there any travelogues or memoirs that offer personal accounts of exploring Greece’s captivating destinations?#

A3: To embark on a literary journey through Greece’s diverse regions, dive into travelogues like “A Greek Summer” by Jeffrey Eugenides, “The Olive Tree” by Carol Drinkwater, and “In Search of Homer” by Tim Mackintosh-Smith. These narratives recount personal experiences, encounters with locals, and reflections on the beauty and significance of Greece’s landscapes and cultural treasures.

Q4: What Greek mythology-inspired books would you recommend for readers interested in the ancient legends and gods of Greece?#

A4: To delve into the realm of Greek mythology, explore books like “Mythos” by Stephen Fry, “Circe” by Madeline Miller, and “The Song of Achilles” by Madeline Miller. These captivating tales reimagine classic myths, bringing to life the gods, heroes, and creatures of ancient Greece with vivid storytelling and modern interpretations.

Q5: Are there any Greek children’s books that would be suitable for young readers to discover Greece’s culture and history?#

A5: For young readers eager to learn about Greece, consider books like “The Children of the King” by Sonia Levitin, “The Golden Fleece” by Padraic Colum, and “The Minotaur” by Nonny Hogrogian. These beautifully illustrated books adapt Greek myths and historical events into engaging and accessible stories, sparking a love for Greek culture in young minds.
