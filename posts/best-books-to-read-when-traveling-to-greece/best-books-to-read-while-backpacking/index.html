Best Books to Read While Backpacking#

Strolling through nature and immersing yourself in the written word simultaneously create a uniquely enriching and enveloping experience for outdoor enthusiasts like us. Whether you’re seeking respite during a peaceful lakeside rest or shelter from inclement weather in your tent, the right book can elevate your backpacking journey to an extraordinary odyssey.

How to Choose the Best Books to Read While Backpacking?#

Selecting the perfect backpacking companions—your books—demands careful consideration. Prioritize lightweight tomes that won’t strain your pack’s weight capacity, and opt for editions designed for durability to withstand the rigors of outdoor conditions. Pack publications that align with your interests and resonate with the journey’s essence: if you’re traversing a history-rich region, embrace books delving into the area’s past; for nature-based adventures, immerse yourself in narratives of other explorers.

Best Books to Read While Backpacking: Recommendations for Unforgettable Adventures#

  • “Wild” by Cheryl Strayed: This memoir chronicles Strayed’s arduous yet empowering solo hike along the Pacific Crest Trail. An inspiring tale of self-discovery and resilience.
  • “Tracks” by Robyn Davidson: An account of Davidson’s remarkable 1,700-mile trek across the Australian desert with four camels as companions. A testament to determination and communion with nature.
  • “In Patagonia” by Bruce Chatwin: Immerse yourself in Chatwin’s evocative prose as he journeys through the remote and awe-inspiring landscapes of Patagonia. A blend of travelogue, history, and cultural exploration.
  • “The Dharma Bums” by Jack Kerouac: Join Kerouac and his fellow adventurers on their wanderings through the mountains and forests of California. A celebration of spontaneity, spirituality, and the beauty of the natural world.
  • “Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance” by Robert M. Pirsig: Embark on a philosophical journey with Pirsig as he explores the intersection of reason and intuition while traversing the American West on a motorcycle. A thought-provoking blend of philosophy, travel, and self-discovery.

Additional Tips for a Memorable Reading Experience#

  • Pack Light: Prioritize lightweight books that won’t burden your pack. Consider e-readers or audiobooks for an even lighter load.
  • Protecting Your Books: Shield your literary companions from the elements with waterproof cases or plastic bags.
  • Selecting the Right Moment: Whether it’s a tranquil lakeside rest or a cozy spot in your tent, choose reading moments that enhance your connection with nature’s beauty.
  • Engage with Your Surroundings: Allow the environment to inspire your reading experience. Take breaks to savor the vistas, listen to nature’s symphony, and reflect on the book’s themes within the context of your surroundings.

With the right selections, reading while backpacking becomes an immersive experience that deepens your connection with the literary world and the natural splendor surrounding you. Embrace the power of the written word as your steadfast companion on your backpacking adventures, leaving you with indelible memories and a profound appreciation for the transformative power of storytelling. The books on this list have been carefully selected based on both their literary merit and their suitability for backpacking trips. They are a diverse group of books that offer something for everyone, from classic novels to inspiring memoirs to thrilling adventures.

Book Reviews#

Wild: From Lost to Found on the Pacific Crest Trail by Cheryl Strayed

Review: Wild is a powerful and inspiring memoir about one woman’s journey of self-discovery. After a series of personal losses, Strayed decides to hike the Pacific Crest Trail, a 2,650-mile journey that takes her through some of the most beautiful and challenging terrain in the United States. Along the way, she learns to face her grief, find strength, and overcome her fears.

Key Takeaway: Wild is a story of resilience, hope, and the power of nature to heal. It is a must-read for anyone who loves backpacking or is looking for an inspiring story of personal transformation.

A Walk in the Woods: Rediscovering America on the Appalachian Trail by Bill Bryson

Review: A Walk in the Woods is a hilarious and heartwarming account of Bryson’s attempt to hike the Appalachian Trail, a 2,190-mile journey that takes him through 14 states. Along the way, he encounters a cast of colorful characters, learns about the history and culture of the region, and discovers a new appreciation for the beauty of the natural world.

Key Takeaway: A Walk in the Woods is a funny, informative, and inspiring book that will appeal to anyone who loves hiking, nature, or American history.

Into the Wild by Jon Krakauer

Review: Into the Wild is a gripping and thought-provoking story about a young man named Christopher McCandless who abandons his privileged life to live in the Alaskan wilderness. McCandless’s journey is both inspiring and tragic, and Krakauer’s book raises important questions about the meaning of life and the pursuit of happiness.

Key Takeaway: Into the Wild is a powerful and thought-provoking book that will stay with you long after you finish reading it. It is a must-read for anyone interested in the themes of nature, adventure, and the search for meaning in life.

The Dharma Bums by Jack Kerouac

Review: The Dharma Bums is a classic novel about two young men who travel across America in search of spiritual enlightenment. Along the way, they encounter a cast of colorful characters, learn about Buddhism, and experience the beauty of the natural world.

Key Takeaway: The Dharma Bums is a funny, heartwarming, and inspiring book that will appeal to anyone who loves literature, philosophy, or the search for meaning in life.

Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance: An Inquiry into Values by Robert M. Pirsig

Review: Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance is a philosophical novel about a man’s journey across America on a motorcycle. Along the way, he explores the relationship between technology and nature, the meaning of life, and the nature of reality.

Key Takeaway: Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance is a challenging and thought-provoking book that will appeal to anyone who is interested in philosophy, motorcycles, or the search for meaning in life.

Tips for Reading While Backpacking#

Books are like pocket adventures waiting to be discovered on your backpacking journey. They offer an escape from the trail, a chance to explore new worlds, and a source of inspiration that can fuel you forward. Choosing the right backpacking books is essential to your literary journey.

The Perfect Backpacking Book: What to Look For?#

  1. Weight and Size Matter: Prioritize lightweight paperbacks or e-books to save space and weight in your pack.

  2. Genre and Theme: Select books that align with your interests and the vibe of your journey.

  3. Durability: Pick books with sturdy covers and pages to withstand the rigors of your trek.

  4. Readability: Ensure the font size and layout are easy on the eyes, especially for reading in low-light conditions.

  5. Re-readability: Choose books you can revisit multiple times to savor their nuances and insights.

The Art of Backpacking Reading#

  1. Plan Your Literary Itinerary: Curate a diverse selection of books to keep you engaged throughout your journey.

  2. Establish a Reading Routine: Dedicate time each day to indulge in your literary escapades.

  3. Create a Reading Space: Find a comfortable spot in nature to immerse yourself in your book.

  4. Embrace the Unplugged Experience: Disconnect from distractions and let the written word transport you.

  5. Share Your Discoveries: Engage with fellow backpackers about your literary finds and exchange recommendations.

Key Takeaways:

  • Select lightweight and durable books that align with your interests and the journey’s vibe.
  • Plan your literary itinerary, establish a reading routine, and create a cozy reading space.
  • Embrace the unplugged experience, share your discoveries with fellow backpackers, and let literature fuel your adventure.

FAQ#

Q1: What are the key factors to consider when selecting books for backpacking trips?#

A1: When choosing books for backpacking, consider factors such as weight, durability, and the book’s ability to inspire and entertain you during your journey. Look for books that are compact, lightweight, and made from durable materials to withstand the rigors of backpacking. Additionally, select books that align with your interests and resonate with the themes of your backpacking adventure.

Q2: How can reading books while backpacking enhance the overall experience?#

A2: Reading books while backpacking offers numerous benefits. It allows you to escape into different worlds, providing a mental break from the physical demands of hiking. Reading can also stimulate your creativity, spark deep reflection, and foster a greater appreciation for the natural world around you. Additionally, books can serve as a source of comfort and inspiration during challenging moments on the trail.

Q3: What genres of books are particularly suitable for backpacking trips?#

A3: The best genres of books for backpacking are those that offer a sense of adventure, escapism, and inspiration. Some popular genres include travelogues, memoirs, nature writing, fiction with outdoor settings, and books that explore themes of self-discovery and personal growth. These genres can help you connect with the natural world, reflect on your own experiences, and gain new perspectives.

Q4: How can I find books that align with my specific interests and backpacking style?#

A4: To find books that resonate with your interests and backpacking style, consider your personal preferences, the destination you’re visiting, and the themes you’d like to explore. Research online, read book reviews, and consult with fellow backpackers or booksellers to discover titles that align with your unique tastes. Additionally, you can choose books based on their setting, historical context, or cultural relevance to the region you’ll be backpacking in.

Q5: What are some practical tips for carrying and protecting books while backpacking?#

A5: To ensure your books remain safe and accessible during your backpacking trip, consider using waterproof covers or dry bags to protect them from rain and moisture. Pack your books carefully in your backpack, distributing the weight evenly and securing them to prevent damage. Additionally, consider using a lightweight e-reader or audiobook player to save space and weight while still enjoying your favorite reads on the trail.
