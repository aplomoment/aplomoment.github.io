Best books to read while traveling solo#

Whether you’re a seasoned globetrotter or a first-time solo traveler, finding the best books to read while traveling solo can be a daunting task. With so many options available, it’s easy to get overwhelmed. That’s where this guide comes in. We’ve compiled a list of the best books to read while traveling solo that will transport you to new worlds, offer insights into different cultures, and keep you entertained for hours on end.

How to Choose the Right Book#

The first step in choosing the best books to read while traveling solo is to think about your destination and your personal interests. If you’re headed to a beach town, you might want to pack a light and breezy read like “The Alchemist” by Paulo Coelho. If you’re more interested in history and culture, you might enjoy “A Short History of Nearly Everything” by Bill Bryson.

Tips for Packing Books for Solo Travel#

  • Choose books that are lightweight and easy to carry. You don’t want to weigh yourself down with a heavy suitcase full of books.
  • Pack a variety of books to keep things interesting. Don’t just pack all travelogues or all fiction books. Mix it up with a variety of genres to keep yourself entertained.
  • Consider getting an e-reader. E-readers are a great way to pack a lot of books in a small space. They’re also more durable than traditional books, so you don’t have to worry about them getting damaged in your luggage.

Our Top Picks for the Best Books to Read While Traveling Solo#

Here are a few of our favorite books to read while traveling solo:

  • “The Alchemist” by Paulo Coelho: This classic novel follows the journey of a young shepherd boy who travels from Spain to Egypt in search of treasure. Along the way, he learns about love, loss, and the importance of following one’s dreams.
  • “Eat, Pray, Love” by Elizabeth Gilbert: This memoir chronicles Gilbert’s year-long journey of self-discovery after her divorce. She travels to Italy, India, and Indonesia, where she learns about different cultures, religions, and ways of life.
  • “Wild” by Cheryl Strayed: This memoir tells the story of Strayed’s 1,100-mile hike on the Pacific Crest Trail. Along the way, she reflects on her life and the challenges she’s faced.
  • “A Short History of Nearly Everything” by Bill Bryson: This book is a fascinating look at the history of the universe, from the Big Bang to the present day. Bryson’s writing is engaging and humorous, making this book a great choice for anyone interested in learning more about the world around them.
  • “The Lonely Planet Guide to Solo Travel” by Lonely Planet: This guidebook is packed with tips and advice for solo travelers. It covers everything from choosing a destination to packing for your trip to staying safe on the road.

No matter where you’re headed or what your interests are, there’s a book to read while traveling solo that’s perfect for you. So pack your bags, grab a good book, and hit the road!
