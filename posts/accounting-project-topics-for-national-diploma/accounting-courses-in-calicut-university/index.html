Accounting Courses in Calicut University#

Calicut University offers a range of accounting courses that provide a solid foundation for students seeking careers in the field of finance. With a rich curriculum and experienced faculty, the university equips students with the skills and knowledge necessary to succeed in this dynamic industry.

Undergraduate Programs:

  • B.Com (Bachelor of Commerce) in Accounting and Finance: This three-year undergraduate program offers a comprehensive understanding of accounting principles, financial management, cost accounting, and taxation. Students gain a strong foundation in accounting practices and are prepared for various roles in accounting and finance.

  • BBA (Bachelor of Business Administration) in Accounting and Finance: This three-year undergraduate program combines business management principles with accounting expertise. Students learn about accounting and finance while gaining a broad understanding of business operations and decision-making.

Postgraduate Programs:

  • M.Com (Master of Commerce) in Accounting and Finance: This two-year postgraduate program is designed for students seeking advanced knowledge in accounting and finance. The program covers advanced topics in accounting, including financial accounting, cost accounting, auditing, and financial management.

  • MBA (Master of Business Administration) with a specialization in Finance: This two-year postgraduate program provides a comprehensive understanding of business management with a focus on finance. Students gain expertise in accounting, financial management, investment analysis, and corporate finance.

Admission Requirements:

Admission to undergraduate programs requires candidates to have completed higher secondary education (10+2) with a minimum aggregate of 50%. For postgraduate programs, candidates must possess a bachelor’s degree in a relevant field with a minimum aggregate of 55%.

Career Prospects:

Graduates of accounting courses from Calicut University have a wide range of career opportunities. They can work in various sectors, including banking, finance, auditing, taxation, and business consulting. Some popular career paths include:

  • Accountant
  • Financial Analyst
  • Auditor
  • Tax Consultant
  • Management Accountant
  • Cost Accountant
  • Financial Advisor

List of textbooks and other materials required for each course#

Calicut University is renowned for its robust accounting curriculum, providing students with a comprehensive understanding of financial principles and practices. To excel in these courses, having the necessary textbooks and materials is crucial. Let’s delve into the specific requirements for each course:

Financial Accounting#

  • Textbooks:
    • Advanced Accounting by Kieso, Weygandt, and Warfield
    • Financial Accounting: Theory and Practice by S.N. Maheshwari
  • Materials:
    • Financial Accounting Standards (FASB) Codification
    • Calculator

Cost Accounting#

  • Textbooks:
    • Cost Accounting: Principles and Applications by Horngren, Datar, and Foster
    • Cost Accounting: A Managerial Emphasis by Charles T. Horngren, Gary L. Sundem, and William O. Stratton
  • Materials:
    • Microsoft Excel
    • Cost Accounting Standards Board (CASB) Standards

Auditing#

  • Textbooks:
    • Auditing and Assurance Services by Alvin A. Arens, Randal J. Elder, and Mark S. Beasley

  • Materials:
    • International Standards on Auditing (ISA)

  • Textbooks:

Schedule of Classes for Each Course#

The schedule of classes for each accounting course at Calicut University is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the subject matter. Each course typically meets twice a week for a total of 60 hours of instruction over the course of a semester. Classes are held during the day and evening hours in order to accommodate the needs of students with different schedules.

The course schedule is available online through the university’s website. Here are the steps to find the schedule of classes for each accounting course:

  1. Go to the Calicut University website.
  2. Click on the “Academics” tab.
  3. Select “Programs” from the drop-down menu.
  4. Click on “Accounting” from the list of programs.
  5. Scroll down to the “Courses” section.
  6. Click on the link for the course you are interested in.
  7. The course schedule will be displayed on the page.

The course schedule includes the following information:

  • Course name and number
  • Instructor’s name and email address
  • Days and times of class meetings
  • Classroom location
  • Office hours
  • Course syllabus
  • Required textbooks and materials
