Exploring the Literary Gems#

Let’s embark on a journey through some of the best books to read in english by indian authors, unearthing hidden treasures and rediscovering beloved classics:

  • Fiction:

    • “The God of Small Things” by Arundhati Roy: A poignant tale of love, loss, and the enduring bonds of family set against the backdrop of Kerala’s lush landscapes.
    • “Midnight’s Children” by Salman Rushdie: A magical realism masterpiece that intertwines the lives of 1,001 children born at the stroke of midnight on India’s independence day.
    • “The White Tiger” by Aravind Adiga: A searing indictment of India’s class divide told through the eyes of Balram Halwai, an ambitious young man from the slums.

  • Non-Fiction:

    • “India After Gandhi” by Ramachandra Guha: A comprehensive and insightful exploration of India’s post-independence history, from the assassination of Mahatma Gandhi to the present day.
    • “The Argumentative Indian” by Amartya Sen: A thought-provoking examination of the role of reason and debate in Indian society, past and present.
    • “The Great Indian Novel” by Shashi Tharoor: A satirical and humorous take on Indian history and politics, told through the lens of a fictional family saga.

  • Poetry:

    • “Gitanjali” by Rabindranath Tagore: A collection of lyrical poems that explore the themes of love, nature, and spirituality, earning Tagore the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913.
    • “The Last Leaf” by Nissim Ezekiel: A poignant meditation on mortality and the enduring power of human connection, set against the backdrop of Mumbai’s urban landscape.
    • “Country of Miracles” by Eunice De Souza: A celebration of the beauty and diversity of India, told through the eyes of a young woman returning home after years abroad.
